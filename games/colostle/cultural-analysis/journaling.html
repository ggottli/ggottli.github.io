Journaling as Gameplay

In Colostle, journaling is not a side activity—it is the game. Each time you draw cards to explore a new room or face a Rook, you receive a prompt rather than concrete instructions. It’s up to you, the player, to interpret that prompt and record the outcome in your journal. In this way, every encounter becomes a writing exercise, transforming simple card draws into a rich, personalized narrative.

Journaling Mechanics

- Draw cards during Exploration and (occasionally) Combat phases.
- Consult the prompt tables to learn what you’ve discovered or the challenge you face.
- Write a first‑person journal entry describing how your character interprets and responds to each prompt.

The official Roomlander Journal provides templates for card logs, maps, and space for freeform writing—emphasizing the journal as both record and creative canvas.

Handwritten vs Digital Journaling

Handwritten Journals

  • Offer a tactile, immersive experience—pen on paper encourages focus and presence.
  • Slower pacing can deepen reflection and give space for sketches and marginalia.
  • Physical pages reinforce the fantasy theme with textured paper and ink.

Digital Journals

  • Enable rapid typing, easy editing, and seamless organization (search, copy/paste, hyperlinks).
  • Allow multimedia integration—images, maps, even audio logs.
  • Can be accessed from anywhere and shared online (e.g., blog posts or social feeds).

Both mediums are valid—choose whichever best supports your creative flow. Many players blend the two, handwriting key entries and later transcribing them digitally for safekeeping.

Reflective Benefits

Journaling in Colostle offers more than just a fantasy diary: it’s a tool for self‑reflection and creative growth.

  • Psychological research shows journaling can improve self-awareness and emotional integration.
    (Faccio et al., 2019)
  • Writing your own prompts and scenes fosters narrative thinking and problem‑solving skills.
  • The solitude of solo play creates a meditative space—every entry is both story and personal expression.

Personally, I tried both methods of play. I started with a physical journal for my adventures and then built a digital platform to continue my adventure on. I thoroughly enjoyed both experiences, but the tactile feel, time for reflection, and ability to organize thoughts better when physically journaling led to a better gameplay experience for me.

Whether you sketch a map of the Roomlands in your margins or type a polished tale on a tablet, your journal becomes a unique artifact of your journey through Colostle’s endless corridors.

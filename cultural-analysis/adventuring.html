Adventuring: Exploration, Combat, and Solitude

Exploration Structure

Colostle is built around alternating phases of Exploration and occasional Combat. In each Exploration phase you draw cards and consult the prompt tables, which are organized by environment: the general Roomlands, the Indoor Oceans, the Cities, or the Battlements atop the walls. Each prompt is a narrative seed—perhaps a ruined library hall, a flooded courtyard, or a hidden alcove—and it’s your job to describe what your character discovers and how they react in your journal. (Angell, 2021)

Combat & Encounters

When you encounter a Rook—massive stone automatons that patrol the castle—you resolve combat with a simple card‑draw minigame. You and the Rook each draw cards; the higher draw wins the exchange, and wounds are recorded in your journal. Victories grant you Rook parts or magical Rookstones to harvest, fueling your character’s progression and story. (Jansen‑Parkes, 2021)

This light combat system is designed to spark vivid fight scenes in your writing rather than bog you down in rules. Every clash becomes a chance to craft an epic journal entry—dodging falling rubble in a grand ballroom or prying a glowing crystal from a defeated Rookling.

Solitude & Reflection

The heart of Colostle lies in solitary exploration. You are the lone adventurer in a castle so vast that even grand citadels and oceans fit beneath its vaulted ceilings. This solitude fosters both creativity and introspection: as you journey through endless corridors, your journal entries become a personal dialogue with the game world and yourself.

Research has shown that solo journaling can enhance self‑awareness and enrich narrative thinking. In playing Colostle, each turn is an opportunity not just to chronicle your character’s exploits but also to reflect on themes of identity, purpose, and discovery. (Wickens, 2025)

The mix of light mechanics and open‑ended prompts turns the solitary solitary into something deeply engaging—your journal is both a record of adventure and a mirror for your own creativity.
